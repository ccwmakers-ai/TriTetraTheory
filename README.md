1. Abstract（要旨）
We propose the TriTetra Theory, a unified geometric framework describing the stability and transformation of complex systems. The theory originates from a fundamental principle: the universe begins not from one, but from two opposing poles. Through a generative process — from bipolar origin to line, triangle, tetrahedron, and finally a nine-point octahedral structure — all complex systems can be described within a single framework.
The nine-point system consists of three points on the real axis, four points on imaginary and sub-imaginary axes, and two central origin points. In a stable state, the vector sum of all nine points equals zero. External stress displaces these vectors; when displacement exceeds a critical threshold, the system breaks down.
